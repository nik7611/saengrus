It's Great to be a Salvationist! Here’s Why!

"The Lord did not set his affection on you and choose you because you were more numerous than other peoples ... but it was because the Lord loved you...” (Deuteronomy 7:7, 8)

 

I want to tell you this morning that I am a Christian by conversion and a Salvationist by conviction. I am a Christian because years ago I made a commitment to Jesus Christ and I am a Salvationist because God used the Army to bring me to Himself. I am a Christian because God in Christ forgave my sins and I am a Salvationist because the people at the Army accepted me as I was and did not pass judgement on my life. I am a Christian because the Holy Spirit entered my life and I became a new person on the day I knelt and accepted Christ as my personal Saviour and I am a Salvationist because God used the Army the help me maintain and grow in that new life experience. I am a Christian because today the blood of Jesus Christ is applied to my life and I am a Salvationist because that is where God wants me today.

One of the defining moments in Salvation Army history is described by Richard Collier in his book, “The General Next To God.” I quote!

Early one morning in May, 1878 – three months before the War Congress met – Bramwell and Railton were summoned to Booth’s bedroom for the day’s instructions. As Booth, who was recovering from flu, paced the floor in a long yellow dressing-gown and felt slippers, Railton scanned the proofs of the pink eight-page folder which was the Mission’s annual report. Its preliminary was bold and succinct:

THE CHRISTIAN MISSION under the superintendence of the Rev. William Booth is A VOLUNTEER ARMY recruited from amongst the multitudes who are without God and without hope in the world….

Bramwell, aged twenty-two, was stung by the imputation. “Volunteer!” he exclaimed, leaning excitedly back in his chair as Railton read this out. “Here, I’m no a volunteer. I’m a regular or nothing!”

Booth stopped dead in his tracks. For a moment his eyes were fixed on his son, expressionless. Abruptly, he crossed to where Railton sat, taking the pen from his hand, He struck decisively through the word ‘volunteer’ and substituted the word “Salvation”  Simultaneously, they scarcely knew why, Bramwell and Railton leapt from their chairs, crying, “Thank God for that!”

 

This was a very special moment in Army history and it was obviously a time when God placed His seal of blessing on this movement. Once this happened the Army spread like a wild prairie fire all around the world in about ten years. Today, there is hardly a country in which The Salvation Army is not being used to bring the love of God and the message of Salvation to the masses. It’s also interesting that if you take a look at the history of the church in Canada you will find that the only truly national revival that ever swept across this land was called "The Salvation Army."

While the portion of Scripture we read earlier in the meeting - Deuteronomy 7:1-10 applied in particular to the children of Israel, we can also apply it to The Salvation Army of today. "The Lord did not set his affection on you and choose you because you were more numerous than other peoples ... but it was because the Lord loved you..." (Deuteronomy 7:7, 8)

We may not be the biggest assembly of Christians in the community and certainly we are not the largest denomination. However, God has raised up this movement for a very special purpose as a witness to Himself in the world.  We are not so presumptuous as to think we are the only witness - but we are a vital part of His witness in the world today. I have seen the success and the failures of many denominations over the years but I am here to say this morning that it is not only great to be a Christian but IT IS GREAT TO BE A SALVATIONIST! HERE’S WHY!

It is great to be a Salvationist because THE ARMY STANDS FOR SALVATION THROUGH THE SHED BLOOD OF JESUS CHRIST.

Our theme and our motto has been and remains, "Blood and Fire."

We make no apology for this! We believe that Christ is still the only hope for this world. We believe that Christ is still the answer to the deepest needs of man!

We believe that man is born as a sinner with a sin disposition, a bent, a tendency toward sin and he ultimately sins of his own free will and is in need of Salvation that only comes through the shed blood of Jesus Christ.

The Army's ministries whether they be in a Citadel like North York Temple, our Family Services ministry, Men's or Women's social services, Correctional Services or whatever are based upon this philosophy of ministry - man is a sinner and in need of Salvation. We are a SALVATION ARMY!

The Army believes and I believe that God made the complete and ultimate sacrifice for sinners like me in Jesus Christ.

The Army believes and I believe that Christ was nailed to the Cross in our place and because of our sin - that His death was a substitution for our own.

The Army believes and I believe that there is absolutely no other way you can get right with God and gain eternal life.

"Salvation is found in no one else, for there is no other name under heaven given to men whereby we must be saved." (Acts 4:12)

The Army believes and I believe that "everyone" regardless of race, social status or spiritual condition "who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved."  (Acts 2:21 NIV)

The Army believes and I believe that when a man or woman comes to Jesus Christ, asking for forgiveness and turning their lives over to Him that they become "a new creation; the old has gone, the new has come." (2 Cor. 5:17 NIV)

The Army also believes and I believe that all who reject God's gracious offer of pardon and Salvation are forever lost and doomed to a place too terrible to think about.  Jesus said, “Then he will say to those on his left, ‘Depart from me, you who are cursed, into the eternal fire prepared for the devil and his angels.’” (Matthew 25:41 NIV)

 The Army believes and I believe that the Lord is extremely "patient with you, not wanting anyone to perish, but everyone to come to repentance."  (2 Peter 3:9 NIV) The Message says, “He is restraining himself on account of you, holding back the End because he doesn't want anyone lost. He's giving everyone space and time to change.” (2 Peter 3:9 MSG) The Army believes and I believe that the only reason that God hasn’t ended this present world and order of things is that He wants to see as many people as possible come to Him for Salvation and New Life!

It is great to be a Salvationist because THE ARMY ADVOCATES A LIFE OF HOLY POWER THROUGH THE MINISTRY OF THE HOLY SPIRIT.

The Army is a major part of the "holiness movement." It was one of the first among the modern day churches to place a strong emphasis upon the work and ministry of the Holy Spirit in the believer's life.

The Army's 10th doctrinal statement is almost a direct quotation of 1 Thessalonians 5:23, "May God Himself, the God of peace, sanctify you through and through. May your whole spirit, soul and body be kept blameless at the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ."

I want to say that I believe the Army's teaching on the Holy Spirit is right on target! I believe that it has the proper emphasis and balance when it comes to the gifts and the fruit of the Spirit. Fortunately, the Army has not gone off on some tangent and come into the possession of a warped teaching concerning the gifts of the Spirit. It is a balanced middle-of-the-road approach!

The Army believes and I believe that God, according to His own purposes and will, gives gifts of the Spirit to us as individual believers to enable us to serve Him effectively. The Army believes and I believe that every Salvationist and Christian has a spiritual gift mix and they ought to seek to discover and put to use for the glory of God those gifts in active ministry. The Bible says, “Each one should use whatever gift he has received to serve others, faithfully administering God's grace in its various forms.” (1 Peter 4:10 NIV)

You will not find in the Army any support for the idea that any one gift is proof that a person has been filled with the Spirit. In fact, it’s my personal opinion that to hold, for example, that tongues is the initial evidence that a person has been filled with the Spirit is the height of spiritual immaturity.

The Army believes and I believe that the only real evidence that a person is filled with the Holy Spirit is the presence and continued growth of the fruit of the Spirit in one's life. It is described in Scripture as “more excellent way” [one that is better by far and the highest of them all—love].” (1 Corinthians 12:31 AMP)

Don't point to your gifts or talents to try to convince me that you have the Spirit of God!  Show me by your love that you have the Holy Spirit! Don’t try to convince me you’re a great leader in the Church by your busy activity, convince me by your loving attitude and behaviour toward others! That’s how I’ll know and that’s how the church will know and that’s how your neighbours will know and that’s how the world around you will know!

It is great to be a Salvationist because THE ARMY HAS SIMPLIFIED THE APPROACH TO GOD.

The Army believes and I believe in a simple and direct approach to God! The Army believes and I believe that we can “…approach the throne of grace with confidence, so that we may receive mercy and find grace to help us in our time of need.” (Hebrews 4:16 NIV)

The Army was born as a reactionary movement! And what did our Founders react against? They reacted against dead, cold and meaningless religion and forms in the established church of the day. It reacted against the misuse and improper interpretation of the sacraments. It reacted against any religious ritual that stood in the way of a person getting right with God.

We still react that way today! The Army is not opposed to the sacraments or to ritual but it maintains that these are not necessary for a right relationship with God. We are opposed to anything that would become a cheap substitute for the grace of God. The Army has sought to point out the "real" as opposed to the "symbolic." The Bible says, "God is a spirit, and his worshippers must worship him in spirit and in truth." (John 4:23, 24)

The Army therefore has simplified the approach to God.  If you look at our eleven doctrinal statements you will find nothing complicated about them.  They are, perhaps, the simplest doctrinal statements of any of the major evangelical churches. They do not go into great theological detail and you can even hold our doctrine book between two fingers.

That doesn’t mean that we are simple!  It simply means that we believe that the path to God is not as complicated as some people want to make out.  You do not have to be a trained theologian or an ordained priest to come to God. You can enter into the very presence of the living God!  Access has been gained by the death of Christ on Calvary!

It is great to be a Salvationist because THE ARMY IS A CHURCH WITH A HEART.

The Army still believes in "soup, soap and Salvation." While we never officially adopted that slogan it was attached to us by those outside our ranks. That’s because the Army believes in a very practical down-to-earth religion.  It simply believes that "If anyone has material possessions and sees his brother in need but has no pity on him" then he has every right to question, "how can the love of God be in him?" (1 John 3:17)

It’s great to be part of an organization that has a deep compassion for the needs of people and seeks to minister to them on all levels - the physical, social and spiritual.  Sometimes that becomes a problem for us when people see us as primarily a 'social service agency' but nevertheless that is our approach.

If working in Family Services five years taught me anything it is that we can become terribly isolated and detached in our Salvation Army from reality.  I was awakened to the fact that I was out of touch with reality when I came up against "a parade of human misery" through the Family Services office. I submit that most of us really do not have any idea what the average man on the street is going through without Christ!

It is great to be a Salvationist because THE ARMY GIVES EVERYONE AN OPPORTUNITY TO BE INVOLVED IN CHRISTIAN SERVICE AND MINISTRY.

I hope the day never comes when you have to have a Bachelor of Arts, Master of Divinity or a Ph.D. to preach and teach the Gospel in The Salvation Army. Some of the most gifted officers the Army has ever had were self-educated men and women who just gave themselves to God as they were, with their faults, failures and inadequacies.

The Army believes and I believe in "the priesthood of the believers."  We believe that we are all called to be "priests unto God" (Revelation 1:6 KJV). The Army believes that the real ministry belongs to the "laos" - the people of God. It is not the select business of a few paid professionals be they officers, Christian social workers or whatever. The ministry belongs to you and to me.

A dear officer, much used of God, whom some of you will have known, Brigadier Charles Watt, said to me many years ago, "What the Army gives us is an opportunity!" I never forgot that! "What the Army gives us is an opportunity!"

We are the Army! We are the soldiers! Without us there would be no Salvation Army in this city! If the Army is failing in its ministry it means only one thing; we are failing in our ministry as individuals! If this church is not what it should be then it’s because we are not what it should be! It’s because I’m not what I should be and it’s because you’re not what you should be!

It is great to be a Salvationist because THE ARMY ALLOWS FOR FREEDOM IN WORSHIP.

Jesus said, “God is spirit, and his worshipers must worship in spirit and in truth.” (John 4:24 NIV) “Those who worship him must do it out of their very being, their spirits, their true selves, in adoration.” (John 4:24 MSG)

It’s true that in some places in the Army, worship has become very formal, cold and "churchy".  We must ever guard against that! That’s the not The Salvation Army way! One of the greatest strengths of the Army has always been freedom in worship. People are invited to participate in prayer, testimonies and in numerous other ways.

I’ve always had a bit of a concern in publishing an order for our meetings in the Sunday bulletin lest some people think that it is cast in stone. While I believe that it’s my responsibility to carefully plan our worship experiences at the same time I want you to know that this is always a "proposed meeting outline." Always we must give way to the leading of the Spirit in our meetings and the freedom of individuals to respond to God's call at the altar.

At the risk of being misunderstood and misinterpreted I want to diverge for a few moments and share a personal concern and opinion. I have great reservations about those who would want us to become "just another church" today! I believe that this whole idea of trying to do away with everything that has historically been Salvation Army will come back to haunt us some day. (In fact, friends, it is coming back to haunt us in this city of Toronto which once a bastion and a powerhouse of Salvation Army ministry and activity!) Yes, there are lots of changes that need to be made but let’s not “throw out the baby with the bathwater”! There are certain distinctives that have made us what we are today and they should be maintained. 

I picked up this interesting quote from an officer in one of our publications who was making a plea to "keep the Army, 'Army.'" He said in part, "...what irks us is that we are now being told that this great movement of the Holy Spirit - The Salvation Army - is just another church.... and we fear that ...the burning desire of so many officers to regard our movements as no more than just another church or denomination, The Salvation Army is casting its great and precious heritage to the four winds, not to mention losing our movement's own peculiar and distinctive characteristics."

I must admit, I like General Orsborne's comment when he said, "The Army is not a church, but a permanent mission to the unconverted."

It’s not that I think that everyone should be a "uniformed Salvationist". In fact, I like the kind of balance you have right here in this Corps. It allows people to come into our meetings and feel welcome and like they belong.

Why should the Devil have the best music? That’s what the early Army leaders asked? I believe very strongly that we need to move into the contemporary sphere of worship and praise and, like our founders, be prepared to use any style and method which will attract individuals to Christ. But I do not believe that it is necessary to for us to disregard our Wesleyan heritage and throw away our roots.

It is great to be a Salvationist because THE ARMY PROVIDES AN INTERNATIONAL FELLOWSHIP UNSURPASSED AMONG THE CHURCHES.

The Bible says, "...if we walk in the light (of Jesus) we have fellowship with one another." (1 John 1:7)

There is something very special about being able to walk into an Army citadel in Halifax, Montreal, Toronto, Winnipeg or Vancouver and feel like you belong!  And, when a Salvationist visits us from London, Hong Kong, Sydney, Johannesburg, Paris, Tampa, Moscow or Whitehorse you sense that these are my brothers and sisters - my friends in Christ. In just a few weeks we’ll have the Boscombe Songsters from England with us for a visit and do you know what? We’ll recognize them right away as part of our international fellowship of Salvationists!

When you stop to think for it for just a few moments you begin to appreciate the magnitude of this movement which God has raised up with its vast ministry network and international community all marching to the beat of the same drum, all fighting for God under the same Blood and Fire flag.  Incredible!

I am glad that I am a Salvationist and an officer and a soldier of The Salvation Army today. I like what the Army stands for today! It may not be perfect!  If it were I would be out of a job and you wouldn’t be allowed in the door! But it’s great to be part of this great movement of God and contribute in some small way to the work of the God in the Salvation of the world.

It is great to be a Salvationist because the Army stands for Salvation by the shed blood of Christ.

It is great to be a Salvationist because the Army advocates a life of personal holiness by the power of the Holy Spirit active and living in our lives.

It is great to be a Salvationist because the Army has made the Gospel message and the way to God as simple as God intended.

It is great to be a Salvationist because the Army cares for the whole man - spirit, soul and body providing soup, soap and Salvation.

It is great to be a Salvationist because it gives people like me and you and opportunity to minister for Christ.

It is great to be a Salvationist because of the freedom of worship the Army allows us in our meetings and activities.

It is great to be a Salvationist because of the tremendous fellowship and sense of community we possess.

When we were stationed in Somerset, Bermuda, there was a little chorus the people loved to sing and sing and sing again. Frankly, I didn’t care much for it and didn’t see it as a particularly helpful or meaningful thing until one day one of the school headmasters called us and asked us if we could give them the words and music for it because all the kids were singing it at school. This is what it said!

 

We are soldiers in the Army

We have to fight although we have to die

We have to lift up the Blood-stained banner

We have to lift it up until we die!

 

Will you commit yourself to doing that today and for the future?
