Prescriptions from God

God’s Antidote For Worry

Psalm 23

 

 

You know the routine! You’re not feeling well and despite everything you’ve been able to do for yourself the ache still persists. So, you make an appointment with the doctor, he examines you, perhaps does some tests, makes a diagnosis and then possibly writes you out a prescription for some medicine. You take it to the druggist and he fills the order and then you take it home and follow the instructions. Hopefully you’ll get better by taking the prescription.

 

This morning I want to begin a new series of Bible messages with you that’s primarily going to deal with the emotional disease of stress. We’re going to look at the seven greatest sources of stress in our lives and then look at God’s prescription or antidote for that particular problem in our lives. We’re going to look at God’s prescription or antidote for worry, busyness, damaged emotions, indecision, dark valleys, hurt and the future and all of the remedies we’re going to look at are outlined in Psalm 23.

 

So, let’s get started! This morning we want to look at one of the great causes of stress in our lives – worry!

 

It was Elizabeth Cheney who wrote these words! “Said the Robin to the Sparrow: ‘I should really like to know Why these anxious human beings Rush about and worry so.’ Said the Sparrow to the Robin: ‘Friend, I think that it must be That they have no heavenly Father Such as cares for you and me.’” (Elizabeth Cheney)

 

Most of us are pretty good at worrying about things! Most of us have our pet worries – our finances, our job, our relationships, our marriage, our kids, our health and on and on we go.

 

Worry is about the most useless thing you and I can do about a problem! Why?

 

Because worry never accomplishes anything! It never solves anything! It’s stewing without doing! It’s like spending an hour on the treadmill – your whip your legs around and work up a real sweat but in the end you are exactly in the same spot on the floor! Worry can’t solve a problem, it can’t change the past and it can’t control the future! It will only make you miserable today. It doesn’t help a bit and it doesn’t work!

 

Worry is unreasonable! It only makes a mountain out of a molehill! It only exaggerates the problem! It just makes you churn the problem around and around in your mind making it bigger and bigger and bigger! To worry about something you can’t change is a useless exercise! It will only make you emotionally sick today!

 

Worry is unhealthy! Your doctor will tell you that! It will give you ulcers, backaches, headaches and cause you to loose sleep! The only creature in all of God’s creation that worries is man! Plants don’t worry! Animals don’t worry! But we do and it makes us unhappy and unhealthy!

 

Some time ago the United States Public Health Service issued a statement presenting statistics on nervous diseases and showing the tendency of the worry habit to shorten life saying, “So far as is known, no bird ever tried to build more nests than its neighbor; no fox ever fretted because he had only one hole in which to hide; no squirrel ever died of anxiety lest he should not lay by enough nuts for two winters instead of one, and no dog ever lost any sleep over the fact that he did not have enough bones laid aside for his declining years.”

 

The Old English for “worry” is the word “to strangle” or “to choke” and that’s what worry does to you. It strangles and chokes the life right out of you!

 

Do you know that you weren’t born worrying! A baby is about as worry free as a person can get! You have to learn to worry. You have to practice worrying until you’re good at it!

 

The good news is that God has a remedy for worry! He has a prescription for worry! He has an antidote!

 

WHAT IS GOD’S ANTIDOTE FOR WORRY?

 

Look at what David says in Psalm 23! “The LORD is my shepherd; I have everything I need.” (Psalms 23:1 NLT)

How does this help? If I believe that God is going to take care of me then I’m not going to worry about it! If I allow God to be my shepherd how does that provide an antidote for stress in my life?

You need to understand the role of a shepherd!

1. A shepherd provides! He provides food and the basic necessities for his sheep.

2. A shepherd protects! He defends against enemies and harm!

3. A shepherd guides! He leads the sheep when they’re confused and don’t know which way to go!

A shepherd corrects! When they want wander and go the wrong way he corrects them!

The amazing thing is that God has promised to do these four things in your life if you’ll trust Him and allow Him to be your Shepherd! Basically He says, “I will provide for you! I will guide you! I will correct you! I am the remedy for worry!” The Bible says, “Like a shepherd, he will care for his flock….” (Isaiah 40:11 MSG)

Actually, He even gets more specific! Philippians 4 says, “…God will meet all your needs according to his glorious riches in Christ Jesus.” (Philippians 4:19 NIV) Notice that it doesn’t say He will meet all your “greed” but all of your “need”! There is a huge difference! If God met all of you wants you’d be the most spoiled brat on the planet! You’d be spoiled and self-centered! He’s not going to give you everything you want but everything you need!

I was listening to an interview with Bill Gates, the richest man in the world, when he was asked about his children and he said, “I don’t think giving the money to the kids would be good for them or society!” So he said he gives them an allowance, looks after their basic needs but he and his wife are not about to spoil them with billions and billions of dollars. He expects them to make their way in the world like anyone else. Smart man! Surely if Bill Gates has the good sense not to spoil his kids we can understand that God knows what’s good for us and what’s not good for us and is not about to spoil us by giving us everything we want.

God says, “I will…” He doesn’t say, “I might!’ or “I’ll think about it!” or “Possibly!” He says, “I will!” Therefore it’s a matter of God’s character. His good name is on the line here! Either He is going to do what He says or He is a liar!

God says, “I will meet all…” What does that include? Mortgage payments? Medical needs? Spiritual needs? Relationship needs? Yes! It includes everything! If God has promised to provide all of your needs, to protect you, to guide you when you’re confused and to correct the problems in your life, what does that leave you to worry about? Not much!

Worry is not only unhelpful, unreasonable and unhealthy, it’s unnecessary! If Jesus Christ is your Shepherd then any time you start to worry you are doubting Him and His promises! Basically you’re saying, “God is not going to keep His promises! God’s not going to take care of my needs! I’ve got to look after myself! Worry comes from a disbelief in and a lack of trust in God!

How Do You Make God Your Shepherd?

It is a rare thing for me to conduct a funeral where the 23rd Psalm is not referred to! I think that’s because people like to assume that the Lord is their Shepherd but the fact is that He is only the Shepherd to those who let Him be their Shepherd. So, how do you do that?

Accept Jesus as your Lord!

Psalm 23:1 says, “The LORD is my shepherd…” (Psalms 23:1 NIV) The Lord can’t be your Shepherd until the Shepherd is your Lord! You can’t have one without the other! That’s just the way it works! You can’t ask Him to be the Shepherd of your life without allowing Him to be Lord of your life!

What does it mean for Him to be Lord of your life? It means that you allow Him to be in control! It means is in charge! Jesus Christ is Lord of your life if you’re allowing Him to be in control and in charge! If He is not then He is not Lord and He cannot be your Shepherd!

To accept Jesus as Lord means three things! Jesus said, “I am the good shepherd; I know my sheep and my sheep know me…..My sheep listen to my voice; I know them, and they follow me.” (John 10:14, 27 NIV) Notice the three words I have underlined! “…know….listen…follow…” You know Jesus, you listen to Jesus and you follow Jesus! You put Him in control!

If you’re worrying about everything you’re wanting to control everything! The root cause of worry is the need to control and worry is an attempt to control the uncontrollable. Worry is human interference with the plan of God for your life! Any time you begin to worry it’s a warning light! You’re trying to control too much! Ever time you start to worry you’re trying to control something that you shouldn’t be trying to control in the first place.

So, who is in control or your life? God gives you two options! Either you can try and be in control of your life or you can let God be in control! God is not going to force the issue but He is going to let you decide. You make the decision as to who is going to control your life. He made you and He knows what is best for you and He has a wonderful plan for your life but it’s comes down to a matter of control. Who is going to control your life? You or God?

If you’re trying to control your life then you’re playing God and playing God is the root cause of all your worry! Every time you start to play God you’re going to worry. When you try to control everything and try to force yourself to control the uncontrollable there’s a little voice inside you that says, “Who are you trying to kid here? You know you’re not going to be able to control everything!” And then you worry and you fret and you stew about things instead of letting God be in control!

Friends, if you’re trying to run your own life without God’s direction you ought to be worried! You have good reason to worry because you are not in control and you never will be! But if God is running your life and He’s your Lord and Shepherd then you know He can control anything that comes your way and you don’t have to worry.

Pray About Everything!

Pray about all of the ‘stuff” of life! Pray about every item on your agenda of life! Take it all to the Lord, the “great Shepherd of the sheep” (Hebrews 13:20 NIV). The God we serve is a God interested in the ordinary stuff of our lives and so the apostle Paul says, “So here's what I want you to do, God helping you: Take your everyday, ordinary life—your sleeping, eating, going-to-work, and walking-around life—and place it before God….” (Romans 12:1 MSG)

What is it that you are most concerned about this morning? You finances? Your relationship with your husband or your wife or your kids or your parents? Some other relationship? Is it your job? Is it your health? Some other item of great concern?

If we could only learn to follow the apostle Paul’s advice! I love this verse! Philippians 4:6 & 7! “Don't fret or worry. Instead of worrying, pray. Let petitions and praises shape your worries into prayers, letting God know your concerns. Before you know it, a sense of God's wholeness, everything coming together for good, will come and settle you down. It's wonderful what happens when Christ displaces worry at the center of your life. (Philippians 4:6&7 MSG) Paul says, “Do not be anxious about anything, but in everything, by prayer and petition, with thanksgiving, present your requests to God.” (Philippians 4:6 NIV)

Did you get that? Everything! Take everything to God in prayer! Not just religious things but every thing on your agenda of life! Every relationship, every need and every concern! Take it all to Him in prayer!

The Bible says, “Give all your worries and cares to God, for he cares about what happens to you.” (1 Peter 5:7 NLT) The New International Version tell us to “cast” all of our worries and anxieties on Him! That word “cast” actually means to dump or unload or to drop!

Prayer is an incredible stress reliever! Whatever you are stressed out about, whatever you are concerned about and whatever it is that is troubling you, you are invited to drop and unload it all on God.

There is one more thing you have to do if you want to deal with the worry in your life!

Take One Day At A Time!

I mean, you can’t do more that one day at a time anyway so just take it one day at a time. The Bible says, “….don't be anxious about tomorrow. God will take care of your tomorrow too. Live one day at a time.” (Matthew 6:34 TLB)

We have some friends who made a commitment to the Lord a few years ago and the husband was healed and saved from a terrible life of drug and alcohol addiction. It didn’t happen all at once and it was a bit of a drawn our and gradual process with many ups and downs. One day his wife said to me, “I just take it one day at a time! Just like the song says,

One day at a time sweet Jesus
That’s all I’m asking of You
Just give me the strength do ev’ry day
What I have to do.
Yesterday’s gone sweet Jesus
And tomorrow may never be mine
Lord help me today show me the way
One day at a time.

Today is the tomorrow you worried about yesterday! When you worry you don’t do anything about yesterday, you can’t control tomorrow and you just mess up today. The future can be bit overwhelming and that’s why God gives us our life timeline in bite size pieces. He just give it to us in little 24-hour tidbits so it’s important to live just one day at a time.

Jesus said when we pray we should ask, “Give us today our daily bread.” (Matthew 6:11 NIV) Overcoming worry is a daily choice! There is no pill that going to stop you from worrying! There’s no seminar or book or tape that going to do it for you! There’s not even a spiritual experience that’s going to cure you from worry! God’s prescription for worry comes down to you and I making a daily choice, a constant choice, in which we say, “Am I going to believe the Lord is my Shepherd and let Him be in control or am I going to try and take charge of my own life and try to handle everything on my own?” Who’s going to control your life? Who’s going to call the shots! If I’m going to be in control then I have lots to worry about but if God is in control then it’s His problem and He is more than capable of handling anything that comes my way!

What has got you worried? What is that that causes you to fuss and fume, toss and turn and wonder how you’re going to make it through life? What is it in the pit of your stomach that you are constantly worrying about?

I don’t know what you’re going through right now but God does and He cares! The Bible says, “…don't worry….your heavenly Father already knows perfectly well that you need them, and he will give them to you if you give him first place in your life and live as he wants you to.” (Matthew 6:31-33 TLB)

Let make a little suggestion to you! When you have your devotions spend the whole week meditating and thinking about Psalm 23. You are going to find that seventeen times in six verses the personal words, “I”, “my” or “me” are used and the word “You” referring to God is used five times. That makes Psalm 23 an intensely personal psalm. It’s about our relationship with God and that’s God’s prescription and antidote for stress. Getting religious will not stop you worrying. In fact, it may even make it worse. You don’t need religion. What you need is a relationship with God. You need a Shepherd to provide, protect, guide and correct you as you journey through life.

This morning I invite you to take the first step if you haven’t done so already and ask Jesus Christ to become your Lord, your Shepherd, your Manager, your Guide.

Wilbur Chapman was asked to visit and try to cheer up a little 10 year old boy who was dying of cancer. He went to the home of the boy and the little boy was worrying about dying. Chapman said, “I want to teach you something. Let me have your hand. The Bible says, ‘The Lord is my Shepherd.’ Every time you start to get worried about what’s going to happen to you just think, ‘The Lord is my Shepherd’ and hold on to your index finger.” Two weeks later the boy died and his mother found him holding on to his index finger.

I don’t know what you are worried about or stressed out about this morning but I do know that God loves you, cares for you, can and wants to help you. “The Lord is my Shepherd!” Say it over and over again and emphasize the different words. “The Lord is my Shepherd!” There can only be one “lord” of your life! Who is it? “The Lord is my Shepherd!” Can you say that with certainty this morning? Is He your Shepherd? “The Lord is my Shepherd!” That is what He wants to be! Will you let Him?
